> **Update — July 2026:** Anthropic restored global access to Fable 5 on July 1 after the export controls were lifted. The note below captures what the interruption felt like while access was suspended. Fable 5 and Mythos 5 share the same underlying model, but Fable is the broadly available version with stronger safeguards; Mythos has more restricted access.

So my experience with LLMs lately — it feels like every other day there's a new model dropping, new benchmarks getting shattered, new companies popping up out of nowhere. Like it's actually wild how fast this space is moving. And honestly it kind of makes sense right, because they're training the new models using the old ones, learning from the mistakes, stacking on top of what already exists. That's just how the whole thing compounds now.

There's actually this article by Anthropic where they talk about exactly that — how they use older models to help build the newer ones. Worth a read. But while going through it I was thinking like... maybe we should slow down a bit? The pace of all this is genuinely hard to keep up with.

And then literally within a few days of that — Anthropic drops Fable 5 alongside Mythos 5. They share the same underlying model, but Fable is the general-use version with stronger safeguards while Mythos is aimed at a smaller set of trusted partners doing defensive cybersecurity work. So the same company that put out that "maybe slow down" article just released what a lot of people were calling a holy grail kind of model. One that's apparently really good at finding bugs, security vulnerabilities, attack vectors — that kind of heavy stuff. Wild timing honestly.

Here's where it got personal for me though — I actually got to use it for a day. I was kind of just free at the office, not a lot going on, so I started messing around with it. Asked it to help design some artifact ideas for a website I'm building. And honestly? It came out really well. I was genuinely impressed. Also fast — which I did not expect.

One thing that hit though is the token usage is absolutely crazy for this model. Way more than what I'm used to. So my workflow kind of naturally split — use Fable for the design and ideation stuff, use Opus for the actual code. That combo worked out pretty nicely.

So next morning I come back to finalize the design part and I just get an error. Tried a few times, swapped models, nothing. Turns out the model got pulled — there's apparently some national security concern around it. Just like that, gone. Something I was genuinely excited to keep using, vanished overnight.

At that point I was back to Opus for everything, which was totally fine—Opus is solid and gets the job done. But there was something weirdly poetic about the whole situation: Anthropic writes maybe we should slow down, drops a powerful model, and access disappears only days later. Just maximum chaotic energy all around lol.

Anyway I've bounced around a bunch of LLMs at this point and Claude is just where I keep landing. Use it for most of my actual work and Claude Code specifically has been really good for the development side. If you're a dev and haven't tried it yet, genuinely worth checking out.

That's pretty much it — just wanted to log the Fable situation because it was a weird experience. Having something click for you, building a workflow around it, and then watching it disappear the next morning. That's kind of just the energy of LLMs right now I guess. Nothing feels permanent, everything is moving, and somehow that's become normal.
